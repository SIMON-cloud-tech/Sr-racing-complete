how stem racing is driving change: impact report 24/25

At the beginning of this year, we launched our new identity: STEM Racing, Supported by Formula 1. This rebrand has taken us into an exciting new era and, as we reflect on a landmark year, we are proud to share our Impact Report for 2024/25.

​A celebration of all we have achieved over the past 12 months - from a phenomenal World Finals in Saudi Arabia to the launch of STEM Racing in Kenya, South Africa, Mali and Togo - we hope you enjoy recapping our incredible journey so far.

world finals 2024

​Held at Dhahran Expo, Saudi Arabia, the 2024 Aramco STEM Racing World Finals brought together 450+ students and educators from 25 countries. Over 55 teams competed in a three-day celebration of innovation, design, and engineering.

    Key Moments:

  • Dates: 23–26 November 2024
  • Winners: Team evolut1on, Germany
  • Special Guest: Fernando Alonso presented awards
  • Engineering Academy: 10 students selected for Komatsu-Williams programme
  • Diversity: 1 in 3 participants were female.

“We spent two years and thousands of hours on this. To win among so many talented teams is incredible.”

Theo Paulsen,
Team Manager, evolut1on (World Champions)

GLOBAL REACH - LOCAL IMPACT

In 2024, STEM Racing gave more students hands-on access to engineering, technology and motorsport than ever before — powered by our partnership with Formula 1®.

    Key Highlights:

  • 13 Grand Prix activations
  • ​300+ students with exclusive paddock access
  • STEM Racing installations at major industry events
  • 700-person pit-lane walk at Qatar Grand Prix

Paddock Tours took place at:
Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, Australia, China, Miami, Monaco, UK, Hungary, Singapore, Austin, Qatar, Abu Dhabi, Mexico

Qatar Grand Prix Spotlight:
One of our largest activations to date – a 700-person pit-lane walk offering students a rare, behind-the-scenes look at the world of F1®.

OPENING DOORS TO STEM CAREERS

In 2024, we expanded access to STEM education by:

  1. Engaging diverse students from across the globe
  2. Partnering with leading teams and foundations
  3. Delivering hands-on experiences that spark ambition

Key Highlights:

  • 120 students joined Make A Mark with Aston Martin at Silverstone.
  • ​Karting activations with Fernando Alonso in China and Spain.
  • STEM sessions in Miami Gardens introducing local communities to engineering.
  • Workshops in Shanghai with Aston Martin, Aramco, Pedro de la Rosa and Jessica Hawkins.
  • Leadership workshop with Aleto Foundation and Aston Martin for underrepresented young talent.
  • Free STEM Racing kits delivered in Soweto with Empower Young Learners and Kliptown Youth Program (KYP).
